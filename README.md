Predictive Escalation Intelligence in Retail Banking Operations
Project Overview

This project explores how escalation concentration within banking operations environments may function as an early-warning indicator for:

operational instability,
servicing cost leakage,
governance inefficiencies,
compliance-sensitive exposure,
and customer attrition risk.

Using simulated institutional banking datasets, the analysis framework was designed to evaluate hidden operational risk patterns through:

escalation analytics,
SLA breach trends,
operational cost modeling,
governance risk classification,
and strategic intervention insights.
Objectives
Identify escalation-driven operational risk indicators.
Analyze servicing inefficiencies.
Estimate operational cost leakage.
Build governance risk intelligence dashboards.
Demonstrate BFSI analytics and controls thinking.
Tools Used
Excel
Power BI
SQL (planned phase)
Key Insights
Escalation concentration may correlate with increased operational servicing costs.
Repeat-contact clusters may indicate governance instability.
SLA breaches may serve as predictive indicators of operational stress.
Compliance-sensitive complaint patterns may identify hidden process risk zones.
